Rewarewa

Where the wild coast meets the forest.
A glass hideaway for two, surrounded by ocean air, black-sand beaches and wide-open skies along the west coast.

The Pod

the essentials, simply done

A short walk through regenerating bush marks the transition from the everyday to calm. The path leads to a private glass pod, set within a protected bird corridor where nature is a constant presence. The calls of tui, kererū and ruru set the rhythm of the place, a quiet reminder that time moves differently here.

Inside, the space is warm, simple and designed for two.
A king bed oriented toward the landscape, a hot shower, flush toilet and essential kitchen amenities are paired with cosy, year-round heating.
Everything you need to live within nature comfortably — without distractions, without hurry.

The experiences that define Rewarewa

coast, culture and wild landscape

A carefully curated selection of three experiences that capture the essence of this place: coast, culture and wild landscape.

Muriwai Gannet Colony & Cliffs

A powerful coastal stop defined by dramatic cliffs and wide-open ocean views — renowned for its seabirds and the raw energy of the west coast. Ideal for striking panoramas, coastal walks and unforgettable sunsets.

Why it’s special: iconic seabirds • cliffs • open ocean

→Explore Muriwai Gannet Colony & Cliffs

Te Henga (Bethells Beach)

One of the west coast’s most atmospheric black-sand beaches — wide, untamed and cinematic.
Perfect for slow walks, shifting skies and that unmistakable “edge of the world” feeling.

Why it’s special: black sand • wild cliffs • west coast mood

Waitākere Ranges

A lush native-forest region with scenic drives, lookouts, walking tracks and waterfalls. The green counterpoint to the coast — quiet, ancient and deeply rooted in the feeling of New Zealand forest.

Why it’s special: native forest • waterfalls • scenic views

→ Explore the Waitākere Ranges

More ways to explore the wild west coast

Beyond the pod, the landscape opens into unexpected contrasts.

Between gentle hills and quiet roads, the Kumeu region invites you to slow down with family-run wineries, long lunches and internationally acclaimed wines. A visit to… Kumeu River Wines, The Hunting Lodge o Westbrook Winery the perfect excuse to savour the west coast’s more relaxed side.

If you feel like adding a gentle touch of activity without complication, the surroundings of the pod offer accessible outdoor experiences that fit easily into the day. From routes Woodhill MTB Park from trail rides to treetop courses and zip lines in Tree Adventures, Here, adventure is gentle, close at hand and unhurried.

Know before you go

The practical details — without breaking the mood

The journeye

A scenic 40-minute drive from Auckland brings you to Helensville, followed by a short 150-metre walk across open farmland to your pod.
Pack light with a small backpack — part of the experience is stepping into nature from the very first moment.

The weather

West coast weather can change quickly. Pack layers, a light rain jacket and something warm for the evenings.
Best moments: golden hour and sunset for coastal views.

The food

Bring your own supplies, or pre-order a locally sourced breakfast and dinner hamper with your stay.
Designed for easy, unhurried cooking and outdoor enjoyment — simple dishes, local ingredients and the kind of meals that linger on the deck, without schedules, without rush.
